## Core Concepts & Frameworks

### Fitzpatrick Skin Phototype System
**Framework**: Classifies UV response and photodamage risk into six types based on burning and tanning tendencies.

**Types**:
- **Type I**: Always burns, never tans. Very fair skin, often red/blonde hair, blue eyes. Highest photodamage risk. Requires SPF 50+ rigorous protection.
- **Type II**: Always burns, sometimes tans. Fair skin. High photodamage risk. Requires SPF 45-50+.
- **Type III**: Sometimes burns, sometimes tans. Medium skin. Moderate photodamage risk. Requires SPF 30-45.
- **Type IV**: Rarely burns, always tans. Olive skin. Low-moderate photodamage risk. Requires SPF 30.
- **Type V**: Very rarely burns, tans deeply. Brown skin. Low photodamage risk. Requires SPF 15-30.
- **Type VI**: Never burns, deeply pigmented. Lowest photodamage risk but still needs UVA protection. Requires SPF 15-30.

**Source**: Fitzpatrick TB. The validity and practicality of sun-reactive skin types I through VI. JAMA 1988;259(14):2099-102. doi:10.1001/jama.1988.03720140045034 <!--hash:8a2f3c1e-->

### Baumann Skin Type System
**Framework**: 16 skin types across four dimensions: Oily/Resistant vs. Dry, Sensitive vs. Resistant, Pigmented vs. Non-Pigmented, Wrinkled vs. Tight.

**Dimensions**:
- **Oily vs. Dry**: Sebum production, barrier integrity, moisturizer needs
- **Sensitive vs. Resistant**: Reactivity to actives, barrier response, product tolerance
- **Pigmented vs. Non-Pigmented**: Melanin response, PIH tendency, hyperpigmentation risk
- **Wrinkled vs. Tight**: Aging signs, collagen density, preventative needs

**Example Types**:
- **OSNT**: Oily, Sensitive, Non-Pigmented, Tight — acne-prone, reactive skin, minimal pigmentation concerns, early anti-aging
- **DRNT**: Dry, Resistant, Non-Pigmented, Tight — dry but tolerant, minimal pigmentation, anti-aging focus
- **ORPW**: Oily, Resistant, Pigmented, Wrinkled — oily, tolerant, hyperpigmentation concerns, aging signs

**Source**: Baumann L. The Skin Type Solution. Bantam; 2006. <!--hash:3b9f2e8d-->

### INCI (International Nomenclature of Cosmetic Ingredients)
**Framework**: Standardized ingredient naming system for consistent product identification and regulatory compliance.

**Purpose**: Ensures ingredient labels use consistent names across products and jurisdictions, enabling safety assessment and allergen identification.

**Examples**:
- Retinol → Retinol
- Vitamin C → Ascorbic Acid (or derivative: Sodium Ascorbyl Phosphate)
- Niacinamide → Niacinamide
- Ceramides → Ceramide NP, AP, EOP (specific types)

**Source**: EU Cosmetics Regulation EC 1223/2009, Annex VI. <!--hash:4c1a9b7f-->

### CIR (Cosmetic Ingredient Review)
**Framework**: Independent expert panel assessing ingredient safety with concentration limits and usage recommendations.

**Role**: Provides evidence-based safety assessments for cosmetic ingredients, establishing safe concentration limits and identifying contraindications.

**Key Assessments**:
- **Niacinamide**: Safe at concentrations up to 10% in leave-on products
- **Retinol**: Safe at concentrations up to 1% in leave-on products; pregnancy contraindication
- **Salicylic Acid**: Safe at 2% in leave-on, 3% in rinse-off; pregnancy caution at high concentrations
- **Hydroquinone**: Restricted/banned in EU; available by prescription in US at 4%

**Source**: Cosmetic Ingredient Review Expert Panel. CIR Homepage. https://www.cir-safety.org <!--hash:5e2b8a4e-->

### Evidence Pyramid (Dermatologic Research Hierarchy)
**Framework**: Grades efficacy claims for skincare ingredients and treatments by evidence quality.

**Hierarchy** (highest to lowest quality):
1. **Systematic Reviews & Meta-Analyses**: Comprehensive synthesis of multiple studies, strongest evidence
2. **Randomized Controlled Trials (RCTs)**: Gold standard for efficacy evidence, controlled comparisons
3. **Cohort Studies**: Observational studies tracking outcomes over time in groups
4. **Case-Control Studies**: Retrospective analysis of outcomes, comparing cases to controls
5. **Expert Opinion & Consensus Guidelines**: Professional consensus based on experience and literature
6. **Manufacturer Studies**: Industry-sponsored research, lower reliability due to conflict of interest
7. **Blog/Social Media**: Anecdotal evidence, lowest reliability

**Application**: Grade ingredient efficacy claims by evidence level. Prioritize RCT-backed ingredients over anecdote-driven recommendations.

**Source**: Burns PB et al. Aesthetic Surgery Journal 2011;31(4):453-60. <!--hash:6f3c9b5f-->

### FDA Cosmetics Regulation (United States)
**Framework**: Distinguishes cosmetics from drugs based on intended use and claims; establishes concentration limits and labeling requirements.

**Key provisions**:
- **Cosmetic definition**: Articles "intended to be applied to the human body for cleansing, beautifying, promoting attractiveness, or altering the appearance"
- **Drug definition**: Articles "intended to diagnose, cure, mitigate, treat, or prevent disease" or affect structure/function
- **OTC drug monographs**: Specific concentration limits for actives (salicylic acid, benzoyl peroxide, hydroquinone, sunscreen actives)
- **Warning label requirements**: AHA sunburn alert, sunscreen drug facts panel

**Source**: U.S. Food and Drug Administration. Cosmetics Guidance. https://www.fda.gov/cosmetics <!--hash:7a4d0c6e-->

### EU Cosmetics Regulation (EC 1223/2009)
**Framework**: Comprehensive regulation with prohibited/restricted substances, concentration limits, INCI labeling requirements.

**Key provisions**:
- **Annex II**: Prohibited substances (hydroquinone, some preservatives like formaldehyde)
- **Annex III**: Restricted substances with maximum concentrations (phenoxyethanol 1%, some preservatives)
- **INCI nomenclature**: Required for ingredient labels using standardized names
- **Nanomaterial labeling**: Ingredients in nano form must be labeled with [nano]
- **Concentration limits**: Specific maximum concentrations for actives

**Source**: Regulation (EC) No 1223/2009 of the European Parliament and of the Council. <!--hash:8b5e1d7f-->

## Ingredient Safety and Contraindications

### Pregnancy Category X (Contraindicated)
- **Oral Isotretinoin (Accutane)**: Teratogenic; absolute contraindication
- **High-dose topical retinoids**: Avoid retinol >0.3%, tretinoin, adapalene during pregnancy
- **High-concentration salicylic acid**: Avoid BHA >2%, especially oral or body treatments

### Pregnancy Category C/D (Use with Caution)
- **Low-dose retinol**: Below 0.3% may be acceptable but discontinue if uncertain
- **AHAs (glycolic, lactic)**: Low concentration (<5%) generally acceptable; avoid high-concentration peels
- **Benzoyl peroxide**: Limited data; consult obstetrician
- **Hydroquinone**: Avoid in pregnancy; safety data insufficient

### Pregnancy-Safe Alternatives
- **Azelaic acid**: Generally considered safe in pregnancy; consult physician
- **Niacinamide**: Safe at typical concentrations (2-5%)
- **Vitamin C derivatives**: Generally safe at typical concentrations
- **Sunscreen (mineral)**: Zinc oxide, titanium dioxide preferred over chemical filters

### Medication Interactions
- **Tetracyclines**: Increased photosensitivity; avoid concurrent chemical exfoliants
- **Thiazide diuretics**: Increased photosensitivity; enhance SPF use
- **Systemic retinoids**: No additional topical retinoids; severe irritation risk
- **Immunosuppressants**: Increased infection risk; avoid invasive procedures
