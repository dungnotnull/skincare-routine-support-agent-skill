# Example 1: Acne Routine for Oily, Sensitive Skin

**Demonstrates**: Full harness flow with framework selection, scoring, and roadmap for acne-prone skin.

## Input

**User Query**: "I have oily, acne-prone skin with some sensitivity — build me a routine"

**Context Provided During Intake**:
- **Oiliness**: Oily, especially T-zone
- **Sensitivity**: Sensitive to harsh products, gets red and irritated easily
- **Pigmentation**: Some dark spots after breakouts
- **Aging**: Early fine lines starting (age 28)
- **Current Routine**:
  - AM: Cleanser, moisturizer (no SPF)
  - PM: Cleanser, moisturizer
- **Concerns**: Acne (inflammatory and comedonal), texture, some PIH
- **Goals**: Clearer skin, fewer breakouts, smoother texture
- **Constraints**: Mid-range budget, moderate time (5-7 minutes), prefers fragrance-free

**Safety Indicators**: Not pregnant, no medications, not under dermatologist care

- **Goals**: Acne control, texture improvement, PIH reduction

### Framework Selection

**Primary Framework**: Baumann Skin Type System
- **Justification**: OSPT profile (Oily, Sensitive, Pigmented, Tight) requires balancing oil control with sensitivity accommodation while addressing pigmentation and early aging.
- **Application**: Guides actives selection (gentle BHAs over harsh scrubs), barrier-supporting ingredients (ceramides, niacinamide), and conservative retinoid introduction.

**Secondary Framework**: Evidence Pyramid
- **Justification**: Acne treatment claims vary widely in evidence quality. Distinguishing RCT-backed treatments (adapalene, benzoyl peroxide) from anecdote-driven options.
- **Application**: Each recommended active ingredient graded by evidence quality, prioritizing Grade A/B evidence.

### Improvement Roadmap

**Phase 1: Immediate Actions (Week 1-2)**

**Priority: 1 | Q-H | Add Daily SPF 50+**
- **What**: Replace current AM moisturizer with SPF 50+ broad-spectrum, use daily
- **Why**: Addresses SPF/Barrier gap (80/100), critical for retinoid users and PIH prevention
- **How**: Apply quarter-sized amount AM as last step, reapply every 2 hours outdoors
- **Timeline**: Immediate benefit, cumulative over weeks
- **Risks**: None; ensure non-comedogenic formula
- **Linked Finding**: SPF/Barrier 80/100 — missing SPF daily use
- **Evidence**: "Fitzpatrick phototype and SPF recommendations" (Photodermatology, 2020)

**Priority: 2 | Q-H | Add Benzoyl Peroxide Wash**
- **What**: Add BP 2.5-4% wash PM only, 1-2 minutes before rinsing
- **Why**: Addresses acne with RCT Grade A evidence (Evidence 88/100)
- **How**: Use PM on non-retinoid nights initially, follow with moisturizer
- **Timeline**: Reduced inflammation in 2-4 weeks
- **Risks**: May dry initially; reduce frequency if needed
- **Linked Finding**: Evidence 88/100 — BP RCT Grade A for inflammatory acne
- **Evidence**: "Benzoyl peroxide in acne treatment" (Cochrane Review, 2018)

**Priority: 3 | Q-M | Add Niacinamide 5%**
- **What**: Add niacinamide 5% serum AM and PM (on non-BP nights)
- **Why**: Addresses barrier support (SPF/Barrier 80/100) and PIH (Skin-Type 78/100)
- **How**: After cleansing, before moisturizer; alternate nights with BP
- **Timeline**: Improved barrier and reduced PIH in 8-12 weeks
- **Risks**: Generally well-tolerated; discontinue if stinging persists
- **Linked Finding**: Evidence 88/100 — niacinamide RCT Grade B
- **Evidence**: "Niacinamide in acne treatment" (Int J Cosmet Sci, 2019)

**Phase 2: Short-term Implementation (Week 3-6)**

**Priority: 4 | M-H | Introduce Retinoid**
- **What**: Add retinol 0.3% or adapalene 0.1% 3x weekly PM
- **Why**: Addresses acne and anti-aging (Evidence 88/100, Skin-Type 78/100)
- **How**: Week 1: 1x weekly → Week 2: 2x weekly → Week 3+: 3x weekly
- **Timeline**: Improved texture and reduced comedones in 12+ weeks
- **Risks**: Retinization (irritation weeks 1-3), may increase acne initially; use SPF daily
- **Linked Finding**: Evidence 88/100 — retinoids RCT Grade A
- **Evidence**: "Retinoid introduction protocols" (JAAD Patient Education, 2019)

**Priority: 5 | M-M | Add Ceramide Moisturizer**
- **What**: Switch to ceramide-containing moisturizer for AM and PM
- **Why**: Addresses barrier support gap (SPF/Barrier 80/100), supports retinoid tolerance
- **How**: Choose with ceramides NP/AP/EOP, apply AM before SPF, PM as last step
- **Timeline**: Improved hydration and reduced irritation in 4-8 weeks
- **Risks**: May feel heavy if skin very oily; choose lighter formula if needed
- **Linked Finding**: SPF/Barrier 80/100 — minimal barrier support identified
- **Evidence**: "Ceramides in barrier repair" (Brit J Dermatol, 2021)
