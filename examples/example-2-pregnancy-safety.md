# Example 2: Pregnancy Safety Check

**Demonstrates**: Safety gate triggering for pregnancy contraindications, appropriate escalation, and refusal to provide routine advice.

## Input

**User Query**: "I'm pregnant and using retinol and salicylic acid — is this safe?"

**Context Provided During Intake**:
- **Status**: Pregnant, second trimester
- **Current Products**: Retinol 0.5% serum, salicylic acid 2% toner
- **Concerns**: Just learned retinoids might not be safe during pregnancy, worried about baby
- **Goals**: Want to know if current routine is safe, what to use instead

**Safety Indicators**: Pregnancy (second trimester), no other medications, not under dermatologist care

## Output (Key Sections)

- **Request Type**: Contraindication check / pregnancy safety
- **User Status**: Pregnant (second trimester)
- **Current Actives**: Retinol 0.5%, Salicylic Acid 2%
- **Concerns**: Safety of current routine for pregnancy
- **Goals**: Determine safety, find alternatives if needed

### Safety Screen: BLOCKING — PROFESSIONAL REFERRAL REQUIRED

**Red Flag Triggered**: Pregnancy + high-dose retinoid + salicylic acid

**Urgency Level**: ROUTINE (not urgent, but requires professional guidance before continuing)

**Recommended Provider**: Obstetrician or dermatologist specializing in prenatal care

**Guidance Provided**:

Thank you for disclosing your pregnancy status. I'm not able to provide guidance on your current skincare routine because:

1. **Retinoids (including retinol at 0.5%)**: Current evidence suggests high-dose topical retinoids should be avoided during pregnancy. While systemic absorption from topical products is lower than oral retinoids (like isotretinoin/Accutane), the conservative approach recommended by obstetric guidelines is to avoid retinoids during pregnancy when possible.

2. **Salicylic Acid 2%**: Low-concentration salicylic acid (as in OTC products at 2%) is generally considered acceptable for topical use during pregnancy, but I recommend confirming this with your obstetrician, especially if used extensively or over large body areas.

**What You Should Do**:

1. **Consult your obstetrician**: Bring your current products (or photos of ingredient labels) to your next prenatal appointment. Ask specifically about:
   - The safety of continuing retinol during pregnancy
   - Appropriate alternatives for your skin concerns
   - Whether salicylic acid 2% is acceptable for your situation

2. **Discontinue retinol temporarily**: Until you speak with your obstetrician, consider pausing the retinol. If you're concerned about skin changes without it, that's a good topic to discuss with your provider.

3. **Pregnancy-safe skincare generally includes**:
   - Gentle cleansing (fragrance-free, mild cleansers)
   - Sun protection (SPF 30+, mineral preferred)
   - Hydration (basic moisturizers)
   - Some ingredients like azelaic acid (discuss with your provider)

**Why I'm Referring You to a Professional**:

Your obstetrician knows your complete medical history, can evaluate your specific situation, and can provide personalized guidance that I cannot offer. Pregnancy skincare decisions should involve your healthcare team.

**Resources**:
- American College of Obstetricians and Gynecologists (ACOG): https://www.acog.org
- Bring your product labels to your appointment for specific guidance
